Student clubs and other activities

The Department of Computing and Technology supports various clubs and social activities for our students. The clubs are empowered by students who share an interest in specific topics such as algorithms, programming, robotics, cybersecurity, and games. The departmental activities enhance the academic experience of our students by providing opportunities for interaction with peers and faculty members outside of the staffroom.

Google Developer Student Club
(GDSC)

Google Developer Student Clubs are university-based community groups for students interested in Google developer technologies.
Students from all undergraduate or graduate programs with an interest in growing as a developer are welcome.The Club creates an environment where Students meet and learn with other budding developers and build solutions for local businesses and communities with Google technology.
This is a platform for new learners to build great solutions for local problems so as to advance their skills, career, and network. Above all to enable students to give back to their community by helping others learn.

Student Research & Projects

Storm Chaser Rover


The rover collects data or picks information from the environment for a given perimeter using the different sensors attached to it. This data is stored in a database from which a dataset is generated. The data set is analyzed by a predictive algorithm to predict weather patterns and identify objects surrounding it. The rover can be controlled using a mobile application (human intervention) and can be switched to Auto mode; in this mode, it is the ability to navigate and avoid obstacles on its own(no human intervention).

Alcohol Detector


This project interfaces an alcohol sensor with Arduino. The detector tests the alcohol level using breath in order to calculate the level of alcohol in the blood using an MQ3 sensor, a 16 x 2 LCD for displaying the PPM value of alcohol, and an LM358 IC for converting the alcohol level sensor output to digital.

Arduino Radar System


Based on an object-detecting technique that makes use of microwaves, the project is a prototype of a radar system that can detect both moving and stationary objects. Identified objects reflect radio waves or microwaves that are sent by the radar antenna. The project also has an alarm system embedded in it that creates an alert once an object has been detected.
